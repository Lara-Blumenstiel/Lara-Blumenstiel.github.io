Hello, I'm Lara

Questions about the living creatures around us are what first drew me to biology. More broadly, the ability to answer my own questions is what drew me to science. Studying animal behavior and ecology, especially in urban environments, allows me to investigate what we see every day and often take for granted.

About Me

I hold a bachelors degree from universtity of constance. For my thesis project I organised and conducted an observational study on social influences on foraging behavior in white storks. Before my thesis I worked at the Max-Planck Institute of Animal Behavior in Constance with Dr. Andrea Flack. We did.... Currently I am enrolled in the Mastersprogramm at the University of Zurich. My research is focussed on cognitive flexibility in a recent urban adapter, the sulphur-crested cockatoo. To study cognition in wild SC cockatoos I designed and built puzzle-boxes with integrated touchscreens. If the screen is clicked correctly the machine automatically dispenses an almond as a reward. My goal is to use experimental paradigms, that have been developed in the lab, to study animals in their natural habitat.
