Hello, I'm Lara

Questions about the living creatures around us are what first drew me to biology. More broadly, the ability to answer my own questions is what drew me to science. Studying animal behavior and ecology, especially in urban environments, allows me to investigate what we see every day and often take for granted.

About Me

  • I hold a bachelors degree from the university of constance. For my thesis project I organised and conducted an observational study on social influences on foraging behavior in white storks. From 2021-2024 I worked as a student assistant at the Max-Planck Institute of Animal Behavior with Dr. Andrea Flack.
  • Currently I am enrolled in the Mastersprogramm at the University of Zurich and supervised by Prof. Dr. Lucy Aplin. My research is focussed on cognitive flexibility in a recent urban adapter, the sulphur-crested cockatoo. To study cognition in wild sulphur-crested cockatoos I designed and built a puzzle-box with integrated touchscreen and feeding mechanism. If the birds click on the screen correctly the puzzle-box automatically dispenses an almond as a reward. My goal is to adapt lab-developed experimental paradigms to study animals in their natural habitats. This reserach project is supported by a scholarship from Science Alumni UZH.
